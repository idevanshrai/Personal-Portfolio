Arizona Desert

The Sonoran Desert is a hot desert in North America and ecoregion that covers the northwestern Mexican states of Sonora, Baja California, and Baja California Sur, as well as part of the southwestern United States. It is the hottest desert in both Mexico and the United States. Moisture-laden air from the Gulf of California and the eastern Pacific Ocean appears in July, bringing more than two months of irregular but sometimes heavy thundershowers that are locally referred to as the “summer monsoon.”

Nature Mountain

Rocky Mountains

The Rocky Mountains, spanning 4,800 kilometers, are a symbol of natural splendor, boasting diverse ecosystems and stunning vistas. Home to wildlife such as bears and elk, the Rockies offer abundant outdoor activities, from hiking to skiing, captivating nature lovers and adventurers alike. Conservation is paramount, with efforts focused on combating climate change and preserving habitats. The Rockies hold deep cultural significance, serving as a historical landmark and spiritual site for Indigenous peoples. With their majestic peaks and rich biodiversity, the Rockies provide a sanctuary for those seeking solace in nature and a playground for outdoor enthusiasts.
